About Dolphins

A dolphin is an aquatic mammal within the infraorder Cetacea.

Dolphin species belong to the families Delphinidae (the oceanic dolphins)

Dolphins range in size from the 1.7-metre-long (5 ft 7 in) and 50-kilogram

Dolphins are widespread. Most species prefer the warm waters of the tropic zones

Dolphins are sometimes hunted in places such as Japan, in an activity known as dolphin drive hunting
